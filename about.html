About Me

I am currently a student in the coding bootcamp at UNC Charlotte. I look foward to pursuing this new career path not only becuase it presents a challenge as I learn this skill, but because it allows me to be hands-on and creative. I have always been drawn to getting my hands dirty and creative expression, whether through carpentry work or music, and I believe the skills I am acquiring now allow me to continue to do just that.

In recent years I have spend considerable time as a freelancing residential handyman and painter as well as a cabinet installer and finish carpenter. It was through this journey that I began to realize what I like and dislike in various workplace environments. As such, I am really drawn to the idea of working in a team environment that is collaborative and creative in nature, but also changes frequently depending on the needs of clients or an employer.

I am a full stack web developer inspired by innovation. Professional and personal experience drive my curiosity and the desire to improve who I am and what I do. From balancing work and school, to freelancing as a handyman and working in construction, I understand that hard work is necessary to transform an idea into a high-quality product. Moreover, whether by learning the fundamentals of guitar, or improvisation on the saxophone, music has expanded my creative potential and discipline as well. Naturally, coding is where this creativity, determination, and passion meet. These experiences have equipped me with the tools to learn, adapt, and thrive in this constantly evolving industry.
